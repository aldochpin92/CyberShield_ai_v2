IT 643 Final Project Guidelines and Rubric
Overview
You work for Strategic Security Consulting Group (SSCG). Recently, one of your clients located in Los Angeles, North Star Software Developers (NSSD), called with a big problem. NSSD’s strategic security goals include protecting the software development process and code as well as sensitive client information from internal and external breaches. Some of its network servers were compromised, resulting in the possible loss of personal information and credit card numbers of purchasers of the company’s software products. It is not presently known whether this attack came from inside or outside the company. NSSD is currently dealing with this problem as best as it can, but it needs your company to help prevent this from happening again in the future.

You have been assigned to create a training manual for NSSD’s IT personnel in which you test, describe, and recommend a variety of network security tools that, when used properly, will mitigate both the risk of future breaches and the effects of any breach that occurs.

You will also create an executive overview that describes the appropriate areas of concern from your research in this course. This should be written in language that can be understood by a nontechnical audience of company management and stakeholders.

In this assignment, you will demonstrate your mastery of the following course outcomes:

Interpret results of network and vulnerability scans for identifying security vulnerabilities
Implement appropriate methods that protect against and address network threats and vulnerabilities using appropriate risk mitigation techniques
Categorize the severity of security incidents using industry best practices for determining the appropriate immediate response
Recommend timely countermeasures that minimize the consequences of current security incidents
Communicate response and mitigation strategies that align with an organization’s strategic goals
Directions
Specifically, you must address the following rubric criteria:

Network Assessment and Defense Training Manual:

Executive Overview
Purpose: Create a brief description of the purpose of the manual that is in consumable terms for the stakeholders. Include the company’s strategic security goals and the value of the policy changes or updates.
Network Defense Assessment: Assess the role of network defense methods and strategies for securing the network. Support your findings with research and experience from the lab exercises.
Mitigation Assessment: Assess the role of mitigation methods and strategies in reducing security risk. Support your findings with research and experience from the lab exercises.
Incident Response Assessment: Assess the role of incident response methods and strategies in minimizing the impact of breaches. Support your findings with research and experience from the lab exercises.
Training Manual
For each section of the training manual, develop guidelines for employees to select and employ specific software, tools, and methods to address the elements of the section topic. Your guidelines serve as recommendations of specific tools and criteria for determining when to use these tools. Each section should include relevant screenshots to support the instructions.

Traffic Analysis: Based on the labs used for traffic analysis, address the tools, methods, and techniques related to traffic analysis. Be sure to include screenshots for the output elements that support the following areas:
Packet Capturing Tools: Create guide materials that explain the process of identifying vulnerabilities and potential threats through interpreting output from packet capturing tools.
Alert Response Procedures: Recommend and describe the alert response procedures required for traffic anomalies identified through monitoring network traffic and examining logs.
Firewalls: Based on the labs associated with firewalls, address the tools, methods, and techniques related to perimeter defense using firewalls. Include screenshots for the output elements that support the following areas:
Rule Creation and Firewall Configuration: Illustrate the process of rule creation and firewall configuration.
Segmenting Networks: Outline the steps for segmenting networks. Be certain todescribe the benefits of the segmenting strategies.
Blocking, Allowing, and Filtering Traffic: Describe the process and criteria for blocking, allowing, and filtering traffic.
Implementing Methods for Detecting Attacks: Provide guidelines for the implementation of methods for detecting attacks.
Intrusion Detection and Prevention: Describe the configuration of intrusion detection and prevention systems, specifically addressing the following:
Whitelisting and Blacklisting: Describe the configuration of whitelisting and blacklisting.
IDS Placement: Describe IDS placement practices.
Monitoring, Logging (Auditing), and Alerting: Summarize the key aspects of monitoring, logging (auditing), and alerting using intrusion detection and prevention systems.
Vulnerability Assessment: Address the tools, methods, and techniques related to vulnerability assessment.
Implementation: Document the proper implementation of port scanning, device scanning, and penetration testing and detection for conducting vulnerability assessments.
Identification of Weaknesses: Explain how to interpret the output of a vulnerability scan to identify weaknesses.
Network Assessment: Address the tools, methods, and techniques related to network assessment.
Processes: Illustrate the processes of detecting rogue devices, detecting malicious connections, and viewing network topologies to identify network weakness.
Interpretation: Describe how to interpret the output of network scans to identify weaknesses.
Auditing and Log Collection: Address the tools, methods, and techniques related to auditing and log collection.
Company-Approved Tools: Recommend appropriate company-approved tools for auditing and log collection and include appropriate rationale and justification for selecting these tools.
Summary of Tools: Summarize the key company-approved tools, represented by those used in the lab exercises. In your summary, identify the importance of and rationale for selecting these tools.
What to Submit
Submit the completed Training Manual using the Training Manual Template. Make sure to incorporate all feedback you have received throughout the course, along with adding your executive overview to your submission. For this assignment, you must follow these formatting guidelines: 12-point Times New Roman font, and APA references and citations. Update the table of contents and ensure all bracketed text is removed prior to submission.
